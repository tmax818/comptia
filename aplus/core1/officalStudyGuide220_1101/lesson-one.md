                    <p>Personal Computers</p>
                </span></div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="9a0725ce-12c8-454f-a500-4e19357d534b" id="9a0725ce-12c8-454f-a500-4e19357d534b">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The components of a personal computer (PC) are divided between those that are designed to be
                    handled by the user—peripheral devices—and those that would be damaged or dangerous if exposed.
                    Peripheral devices typically perform the function of input (keyboard, mouse, microphone, and
                    camera), output (monitor and speakers), or external storage.</p>
                <p>The system case/chassis houses the internal components. These include the motherboard, central
                    processing unit (CPU), system memory modules, adapter cards, fixed disks, and power supply unit.
                    Most cases use a tower form factor that is designed to be oriented vertically and can be placed
                    on a desk or on the floor.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text-block ps-pattern-text-block-note"
        data-id="ca350f4b-910c-4d84-8507-149e44cad2a9" id="ca350f4b-910c-4d84-8507-149e44cad2a9">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure>
                <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                    <p>PCs can also be purchased as <span class="cemphasis" data-type="gt-inline-style">all-in-one
                            units</span>. All-in-one means that the internal components are contained within a case
                        that is also a monitor.</p>
                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="1e09bb61-5f14-40bc-aa5b-95a3c7fcf3d0" id="1e09bb61-5f14-40bc-aa5b-95a3c7fcf3d0">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>To perform PC maintenance, you must understand how to open a desktop computer's case.</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>A tower case has a side cover that can be removed by sliding the panel from its housing.
                        Cases might be secured by screws or retaining clips and might have anti-tamper security
                        mechanisms. Always refer to the system documentation, and follow the recommended steps.</li>
                    <li>The front panel provides access to the removable media drives, a power on/off switch, and
                        light- emitting diodes (LEDs) to indicate drive operation. The front cover can be removed
                        but may require the side panel to be removed first to access the screws or clips that secure
                        it.&nbsp;</li>
                </ul>
                <p><br></p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>Below the PSU, there is a cutout aligned with the motherboard's input/output (I/O) ports. These
                    allow for the connection of peripheral devices.</p>
                <p>At the bottom of the rear panel there are cutout slots aligned with the position of adapter card
                    slots to allow cables to be connected to any I/O ports on the cards. These slots should either
                    be covered by an adapter card or a metal strip known as a blanking plate. Uncovered slots can
                    disrupt the proper flow of air around components in the PC and cause overheating and increase
                    the amount of dust in the system.</p>

                    <p>Peripheral Devices</p>
                </span></div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="7abd6b71-0d8f-4c42-bcfd-221f0aa33561" id="7abd6b71-0d8f-4c42-bcfd-221f0aa33561">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>An input/output (I/O) port allows a device to be connected to the PC via a <strong>peripheral
                        cable</strong>. Some ports are designed for a particular type of device, such as a graphics
                    port to connect a monitor. Other ports support a variety of device types. External ports are
                    positioned at the rear or front of the PC through c<span>utouts </span> in the case. They can be
                    provided on the motherboard or as an expansion card.</p>

        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>Interfaces, Ports, and Connectors</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="ed02b727-50a0-4e79-813d-85c09cd380ee" id="ed02b727-50a0-4e79-813d-85c09cd380ee">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>A hardware port is the external connection point for a particular type of bus interface. A bus
                    allows the transfer of data to and from devices. The connector is the part of a peripheral cable
                    that can be inserted into a port with the same shape or form factor. Each bus interface type
                    might use multiple connector form factors. Most connectors and ports now use edge contacts and
                    either have an asymmetric design called <em>keying</em> to prevent them from being inserted the
                    wrong way around or are reversible.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>Binary Data Storage and Transfer Units</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="ae65196e-8540-4907-a2e9-ca419fa4c753" id="ae65196e-8540-4907-a2e9-ca419fa4c753">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>When comparing bus interfaces, it is important to use appropriate units. Computers process binary
                    data. Each binary digit or bit (b) can have the value one or zero. Storage is often measured in
                    multiples of eight bits, referred to as a byte (B). A lowercase “b” unit refers to a bit, while
                    uppercase means a byte.</p>
                <p>Transfer rates are expressed in units per second of the following multiples of bits and bytes:
                </p>
                <ul>
                    <li>1000—Kilobits (Kb/s or Kbps) and kilobytes (KB/s and KBps).</li>
                    <li>1000x1000—Megabits (Mb/s) or megabytes (MB/s).</li>
                    <li>1000x1000x1000—Gigabits (Gb/s) and gigabytes (GB/s).</li>
                </ul>
                <p><br></p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

                    <p>Universal Serial Bus Cables</p>
                </span></div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"

        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The Universal Serial Bus (USB) is the standard means of connecting most types of peripheral
                    device to a computer. USB peripheral device functions are divided into classes, such as human
                    interface (keyboards and mice), mass storage (disk drives), printer, audio device, and so on.
                </p>
                <p>A USB is managed by a host controller. Each host controller supports multiple ports attached to
                    the same bus. In theory, there could be up to 127 connected devices per controller, but to
                    overcome the limitations of sharing bandwidth, most PC motherboards provision multiple USB
                    controllers, each of which has three or four ports.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>USB Connector Types</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="a5a4b649-8dd1-48c1-a412-eca8eb7e499f" id="a5a4b649-8dd1-48c1-a412-eca8eb7e499f">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The connector form factors specified in USB 2 are as follows:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>Type A—For connection to the host and some types of peripheral device. The connector and
                        port are shaped like flat rectangles. The connector should be inserted with the USB symbol
                        facing up.</li>
                    <li>Type B—For connection to large devices such as printers. The connector and port are square,
                        with a beveled top.</li>
                    <li>Type B <strong>Mini</strong>—A smaller peripheral device connector. This type of connector
                        was seen on early digital cameras but is no longer widely used.</li>
                    <li>Type B <strong>Micro</strong>—An updated connector for smaller devices, such as smartphones
                        and tablets. The micro connector is distinctively flatter than the older mini type of
                        connector.</li>
                </ul>

                    A to Type B or Type A to Micro Type B, for instance).</p>
                <p>In USB 3, there are new versions of the Type A, Type B, and Type B Micro connectors with

            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>USB 3.1 defines the USB-C connector type. This compact form factor is intended to provide a
                    single, consistent hardware interface for the standard. The connector is reversible, meaning it
                    can be inserted either way up. The connector design is also more robust than the earlier miniB
                    and microB types. USB-C can use the same type of connector at both ends, or you can obtain USB-C
                    to USB Type A or Type B converter cables.</p>

        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>Cable Length</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="5b9b3e34-d1ec-490c-a265-253943d98009" id="5b9b3e34-d1ec-490c-a265-253943d98009">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The maximum cable length for LowSpeed devices is 3 m, while for FullSpeed and HighSpeed the limit
                    is 5 m. Vendors may provide longer cables, however. Although SuperSpeed-capable cables do not
                    have an official maximum length, up to about 3 m is recommended.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

    <h3 class="gt-block gt-heading b-h3 ps-h3-undefined h3" data-id="fb5de606-123f-4641-9c68-c6d66cf96bcd"

        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>Power</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="a0b6ab19-2df4-458d-831d-b52db0336bf5" id="a0b6ab19-2df4-458d-831d-b52db0336bf5">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>As well as a data signal, the bus can supply power to the connected device. Most USB Type A and
                    Type C ports can be used to charge the battery in a connected device.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text-block ps-pattern-text-block-note"

        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure>
                <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                    <p>Basic USB ports can supply up to about 4.5 watts, depending on the version. A power delivery
                        (PD)–capable port can supply up to 100 watts, given suitable connectors and cabling.</p>
                </div>

<div aria-labelledby="9c08b5e6-cff6-45bb-bf19-2a681c5dce04" class="gt-section b-section-page gt-box">
    <div class="gt-block gt-header b-header-page ps-header-page-undefined"
        data-id="aaa9895f-7f73-44dc-9ba8-895a0f30e246" id="aaa9895f-7f73-44dc-9ba8-895a0f30e246">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-heading section-page" data-id="9c08b5e6-cff6-45bb-bf19-2a681c5dce04"
                id="9c08b5e6-cff6-45bb-bf19-2a681c5dce04"><span>
                    <p>HDMI and DisplayPort Video Cables</p>
                </span></div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="8a6ddc6b-8ac4-4237-aca3-28dd61e347c9" id="8a6ddc6b-8ac4-4237-aca3-28dd61e347c9">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The USB interface supports many types of devices, but it has not traditionally been used for
                    video. As video has high bandwidth demands, it is typically provisioned over a dedicated
                    interface.</p>
                <p><strong>Video cable</strong> bandwidth is determined by two main factors:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>The resolution of the image, measured in horizontal pixels by vertical pixels. For example,
                        1920x1200 is the typical format of high-definition (HD) video and 3840x2160 is typical of 4K
                        video.</li>
                    <li>The speed at which the image is redrawn, measured in hertz (Hz) or frames per second (fps).
                    </li>
                </ul>
                <p>As examples, uncompressed HD video at 60 fps requires 4.5 Gbps, while 4K at 60 fps requires 8.91
                    Gbps.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text-block ps-pattern-text-block-note"
        data-id="470b38e7-4050-4932-be04-792aa5099504" id="470b38e7-4050-4932-be04-792aa5099504">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure>
                <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                    <p>The frame rate in fps is used to describe the video source, while hertz is the refresh rate
                        of the display device and video interface. To avoid display artefacts such as ghosting and
                        tearing, the refresh rate should match the frame rate or be evenly divisible by it. For
                        example, if the frame rate is 60 fps and the refresh rate is 120 Hz, the video should play
                        smoothly.</p>
                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="7550da5a-f9dd-4c21-97c4-1772551b6018" id="7550da5a-f9dd-4c21-97c4-1772551b6018">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>Computer displays are typically of the liquid crystal display (LCD) t<span class="cemphasis"
                        data-type="gt-inline-style">hin film transistor (TFT) type. </span>Each pixel in a color LCD
                    comprises cells with filters to generate the three additive primary colors red, green, and
                    blue<span> (RGB</span>). Each pixel is addressed by a transistor to vary the intensity of each
                    cell, therefore creating the gamut (range of colors) that the display can generate. The panel is
                    illuminated by a light<span>-</span>emitting diode (LED) array or backlight.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text-block ps-pattern-text-block-note"
        data-id="b8c38280-0c41-4e49-bdbd-a070ad0fddaf" id="b8c38280-0c41-4e49-bdbd-a070ad0fddaf">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure>
                <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                    <p><span class="cemphasis" data-type="gt-inline-style">An LCD/TFT is often just referred to as a
                        </span>flat<span>-</span>panel display. They are also called LED displays after the
                        backlight technology (older flat panels use fluorescent tube backlights). Premium
                        flat<span>-</span>panel monitors are of the organic LED (OLED) type. This means that each
                        pixel is its own light source. This allows for much better contrast and color fidelity.</p>
                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <h3 class="gt-block gt-heading b-h3 ps-h3-undefined h3" data-id="50d781c6-3b79-4cdb-9981-0a9bfef1d1bc"
        id="50d781c6-3b79-4cdb-9981-0a9bfef1d1bc">
        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>High-Definition Multimedia Interface</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="a1a62c06-91a4-4a33-af8c-57e7edd443b5" id="a1a62c06-91a4-4a33-af8c-57e7edd443b5">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>The <span id="a5c33c44-d295-4f78-9dc8-82765ea91691" class="gt-note definition"
                        data-note="<p> Digital audio/video interface developed for use on both consumer electronics and computer equipment.</p>"
                        data-ref-id="41949243-1da2-41f1-88ca-70a480ee16e3"
                        data-external-id="2df2f3ca-3dff-48a6-aaaa-24a137d4e981"
                        data-gtnote="1691267324983--0.03215413286360436"><span class="glossary-term"
                            data-type="gt-inline-style"><strong>High-Definition Multimedia Interface
                                (HDMI)</strong></span></span> is the most widely used video interface. It is
                    ubiquitous on consumer electronics, such as televisions, games consoles, and Blu-ray players as
                    well as on monitors designed for use with PCs. HDMI supports both video and audio, plus remote
                    control and digital content protection (HDCP). Updates to the original HDMI specification have
                    introduced support for high resolutions, such as 4K and 8K, and gaming features, such as the
                    ability to vary the monitor refresh rate to match the frame rate of the video source.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text-block ps-pattern-text-block-note"
        data-id="fcf72cd4-a0a1-4172-b9aa-8560f5d98518" id="fcf72cd4-a0a1-4172-b9aa-8560f5d98518">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure>
                <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                    <p>Support for audio is useful because most TVs and monitors have built-in speakers. The video
                        card must have an audio chipset for this to work, however.</p>
                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="8a010eef-5bfe-4527-a706-50dca3ee61a6" id="8a010eef-5bfe-4527-a706-50dca3ee61a6">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>There are full-size (Type A), mini (Type C), and micro (Type D) connectors, all of which are
                    beveled to ensure correct orientation.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-image ps-pattern-image-undefined"
        data-id="bb9e1a5f-c1a3-400e-83e1-af72a9953c96" id="bb9e1a5f-c1a3-400e-83e1-af72a9953c96">

            <figure><img src="assets/7410-1677002165921.png"

                    class="gt-el-image gt-el-image-image e-image-default"
                    data-external-id="c735328e-0cb0-4da1-ab37-3264e0cdf7d3">
                <div class="gt-el-caption gt-el-caption-caption e-caption-default">
                    <p>HDMI connector and port on the left and mini-HDMI connector and port on the right. (Image

                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="64760613-8a11-4170-beb1-33e7ad1111aa" id="64760613-8a11-4170-beb1-33e7ad1111aa">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>HDMI cable is rated as either Standard (Category 1) or High Speed (Category 2). High Speed cable
                    supports greater lengths and is required for v1.4 features, such as 4K and refresh rates over 60
                    Hz. HDMI versions 2.0 and 2.1 specify Premium High Speed (up to 18 Gbps) and Ultra High Speed
                    (up to 48 Gbps) cable ratings.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <h3 class="gt-block gt-heading b-h3 ps-h3-undefined h3" data-id="9e2ae9d9-6aa2-448d-b7ae-d847ba912bf1"
        id="9e2ae9d9-6aa2-448d-b7ae-d847ba912bf1">
        <div class="gt-content"><span>
                <p>DisplayPort Interface</p>
            </span></div>
    </h3>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="3d34d66c-6d42-4091-b039-df063619643b" id="3d34d66c-6d42-4091-b039-df063619643b">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>HDMI was developed by consumer electronics companies and requires a royalty to use. <span
                        id="1fe57f41-b7ff-490c-8d70-0388fbfc6266" class="gt-note definition"
                        data-note="<p> Digital audio/video interface developed by VESA. DisplayPort supports some cross-compatibility with DVI and HDMI devices.</p>"
                        data-ref-id="c811fa41-f62f-4b87-801c-f2bf4fb212f4"
                        data-external-id="77aa16c9-8d08-4c3e-87b6-1ea8abd48b60"
                        data-gtnote="1691267324983--0.7537657397835582"><span class="glossary-term"
                            data-type="gt-inline-style"><strong>DisplayPort</strong></span></span> was developed as
                    a royalty-free standard by the Video Electronics Standards Association (VESA), which is an
                    organization that represents PC graphics adapter and display technology companies. DisplayPort
                    supports similar features to HDMI, such as 4K, audio, and content protection. There are
                    full-size DP++ and MiniDP/mDP port and connector types, which are keyed against incorrect
                    orientation.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-image ps-pattern-image-undefined"
        data-id="d769c724-1859-46e4-9050-26b510857f8d" id="d769c724-1859-46e4-9050-26b510857f8d">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <figure><img src="assets/5179-1677002165964.jpg"
                    alt="Illustration of a DP++ DisplayPort port and connector."
                    class="gt-el-image gt-el-image-image e-image-default"

                <div class="gt-el-caption gt-el-caption-caption e-caption-default">
                    <p>A DP++ DisplayPort port and connector. (Image ©123RF.com)</p>
                </div>
            </figure>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
        data-id="d40381c1-34d7-446e-a1a9-e6bff81860ff" id="d40381c1-34d7-446e-a1a9-e6bff81860ff">
        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p>Bandwidth can be allocated in bonded lanes (up to four). The bitrate of each lane was originally
                    2.7 Gbps but is now (with version 2.0) up to 20 Gbps.</p>
                <p>One of the main advantages of DisplayPort over HDMI is support for daisy-chaining multiple
                    monitors to the same video source. Using multiple monitors with HDMI requires one video card
                    port for each monitor.</p>

    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"

        <div class="gt-content">
            <div class="gt-el-text gt-el-text-text e-text-default">
                <p><span id="bb1b769c-e7af-4f2b-8954-7c0dda7e8567" class="gt-note definition"

    <div class="gt-block gt-pattern b-pattern-text ps-pattern-text-undefined"
